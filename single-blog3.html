Environmental damage...

30 Oct 2022 Article Prof Oommen Varghese

Environmental damage is an unavoidable consequence of man’s craze for modern life style. Flooding in the Himalayas, gaping hole in the ozone layer, massive oil spill in the Mexican Gulf and wanton destruction of the rain forests in the Amazon are the news that greet us every morning as we tune in our tellies and radios. The environmental damage has reached a staggering proportion as a result of the quest for improvement on our way of life.

We build sky scrapers in the name of urban development, we cut down trees and build tourist resorts in the name of eco-tourism, we manipulate our crops and vegetables in the name of genetic engineering and we have even gone to space and littered it with spy satellites and the attendant debris in the name of space exploration.

We take it for granted that we are the only custodians of the planet and that we have the absolute power and right over every object on it.

In the beginning of the civilization, humans were at the mercy of the environment. Gradually, the changing life style has altered the scenario: and today the planet Earth is at our mercy. What an irony? What a predicament ?

The result of this reversal is so catastrophic that we hardly have pure drinking water because our seas and rivers are contaminated; we hardly breath any fresh air because it is toxic. The environment that envelopes us is like a green house in which we humans lead a life similar to that of the tomatoes, cucumbers and cabbages that are fed on synthetic mineral diet, supplemented by pesticides and insecticides. The ultimate result of our so-called modern living! Finally, William Wordsworth, an ardent lover of Nature observed in one of his poems that “Nature did never betray the heart that loved her.” Let us be a bit more considerate to Mother Earth; let us preserve it for ourselves and for the generations to come. Let us put an end to our wanton behavior and insatiable greed before the prophetic apocalypse comes true. I am reminded of the following words of wisdom: “Better be late than the late”.

Thank You!
